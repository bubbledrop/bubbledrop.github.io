A bit about the song:
Israel has been in a state of war pretty much since its inception in 1948. I won't go into too much detail, it's a long and complicated and painful story we can save for another time. The bottom line is that Israel has had to go to war about once a decade throughout its existence.

The writer of this song is a famous Israeli author named David Grossman (you can probably find some of his books in Chinese). He lost his son during the 2nd Lebanon War (2006), which was a rough period for the Israeli public. This song is an extended metaphor poem about his son (and other boys like him), represented by the spring.
The song doesn't complain, but resignedly accepts and laments the reality in which such young people have to die at war. It just hints at the unfairness of the situation without offering any resolution. Over the years, the pain of bereavement has sprouted in Israel a genre of similar 'memorial songs'. They're very sad but I like them a lot!

The singer is a (domestically) venerated Israeli singer named Yehuda Poliker. He sings only in Hebrew (and a bit of Greek), but you may still be able to enjoy the music.

I did my best to capture the feel in the translation. The title is 'How Short in This Land is the Spring'. Hope you like it!
