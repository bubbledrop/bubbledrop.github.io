Message structure

  • An HL7 message is divided into segments, separated by the <CR> (ASCII 0x0d) character. Every segment starts with a 3 character acronym [A-Z][A-Z0-9]{2}, the segment ID.
  • The first segment of the message is special and is called the header. It begins with the letters MSH.
  • The MSH part of the header is immediately followed by 5 characters called the separators or delimiters. They can be customized, but their recommended and most commonly used values are "|^~\&" (minus the quotes).
  • The first character is called the field/composite delimiter. The second is called the component delimiter. The third is called the repetition separator. The fourth is called the escape character. The fifth is called the sub-component separator. The standard defines no further subdivision after sub-components.
