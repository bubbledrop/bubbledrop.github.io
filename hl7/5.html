Message structure (cont.)

  • The fields are labeled per segment. In the header (MSH segment), the letters MSH are the first field, and may therefore be treated as MSH-0. The field separator (the pipe symbol from before) is MSH-1, and the remaining delimiters (^~\&) are MSH-2.
  • A typical header looks something like:
    MSH|^~\&|GHH LAB|ELAB-3|GHH OE|BLDG4|200202150930||ORU^R01|CNTRL-3456|P|2.4<CR>
    Note that the security field (MSH-8) is empty ("unpopulated"), which in general is valid. MSH-9 is the message type field, which determines which segments one should expect in the remainder of the message, what their order will be and what fields they will contain. The order and data types of the fields in every kind of HL7 message are defined by the standard.
  • Segments can either be solitary or part of a segment group. Solitaries or groups are defined as either mandatory, [optional], {repeating} or [{optional and repeating}] (0 or more). This kind of notation is called segment notation. A valid message must adhere to both the sequential order of the segments and the quantitative specifications.
  • Segments starting with the letter Z are called Z segments and are reserved by the protocol for extensions. Proprietary protocols based on HL7 are encouraged to use Z segments for their superstandard data. Similarly, one can extend a segment with additional fields, provided they appear at the end of the standard-specified segment.
